2. Energy-Wavelength Conversions

Convert between energy and wavelength using the photon energy equation:

E = hc/λ
where h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s, c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s
E(eV) = 1240/λ(nm) (convenient form)
a. 1 eV → wavelength
λ = 1240/E = 1240/1 = 1240 nm
b. 405 nm → energy
E = 1240/λ = 1240/405 = 3.06 eV
c. 0.5 micron = 500 nm → energy
E = 1240/500 = 2.48 eV
d. 2.2 eV → wavelength
λ = 1240/2.2 = 564 nm
e. 600 nm → energy
E = 1240/600 = 2.07 eV
f. Si bandgap at 300K ≈ 1.12 eV → wavelength
λ = 1240/1.12 = 1107 nm
g. GaAs bandgap at 300K ≈ 1.42 eV → wavelength
λ = 1240/1.42 = 873 nm
3. Monochromatic Light Source Parameters

Fill in the missing parameters using the relationship between photon energy, flux, and power density:

Power Density = Photon Energy × Photon Flux
P = E × Φ
Photon Energy Photon Flux Power Density
1.43 eV 1 × 10¹⁹ photons m⁻²s⁻¹ 229 W/m²
1.12 eV 4.46 × 10²¹ photons m⁻²s⁻¹ 800 W/m²
1.25 eV 5 × 10²¹ photons m⁻²s⁻¹ 1000 W/m²
Row 1 calculation:
P = E × Φ = 1.43 eV × 1 × 10¹⁹ photons/m²s
P = 1.43 × 1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ J × 1 × 10¹⁹ /m²s = 229 W/m²
Row 2 calculation:
Φ = P/E = 800 W/m² ÷ (1.12 × 1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ J)
Φ = 800 ÷ (1.794 × 10⁻¹⁹) = 4.46 × 10²¹ photons m⁻²s⁻¹
Row 3 calculation:
E = P/Φ = 1000 W/m² ÷ (5 × 10²¹ photons/m²s)
E = 2 × 10⁻¹⁹ J = 2 × 10⁻¹⁹ ÷ 1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ = 1.25 eV
